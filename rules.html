Rules
  1. Mini Cryptex 2020 will be a day-long event, and will start at 6:00PM on 21th November 2020 and end at 6:00 PM on 22th November 2020.
  2. A maximum of five members per team is permitted.
  3. The first individual to finish minicryptex will win prizes. In the event that no one finshes minicryptex,prizes will be awarded to the individual who has made the most progress on the leaderboard.
  4. The questions, answers or any discussion related to them must not be posted anywhere. Doing so will result in disqualification. We will provide a platform to communicate with the moderators and ask for hints.
  5. If a question involves typing answers in an answer box, the answers should be typed completely in lowercase, without any spaces, punctuation, special characters. Numerals are allowed.
  6. Every team’s progress will be monitored through an online leaderboard. After each level, the teams are required to type in the answer or something related to it to update their position on the leaderboard. Every level will have a submission format for the portal. Make sure your answers adhere to the submission format.
  7. If it isn’t obvious already, access to the Internet and a device to connect with are important prerequisites.
  8. Any attempt to access levels you have not yet reached or any form of attack on the servers will result in disqualification.
  9. Collaborating with another team is strictly prohibited and may lead to the disqualification of both teams involved. Allegations related to dogging another team will also be looked into very seriously and can have serious consequences.
  10. The organizer’s decision is final in any case.
  11. The event can be cancelled in the case of insufficient participation.
Guidelines
  1. Google is your friend. Usually. You’re expected to use Google and other online tools in your quest to crack the questions. Do keep in mind that Google may give you erroneous results in certain cases.
  2. The questions are open-ended, may have red herrings, cheeky hints and may lead you on wild goose chases. If you are getting nowhere with a certain approach, do consider that you might be on the wrong train of thought.
  3. On the other hand, it could be that the answer is ‘colour’ when you have been trying ‘color’. Check for alternate spelling, names and other such variations.
  4. Clues might be hidden (especially in the first few questions) to reduce wrong trains of attack. Their absence, however, does not indicate anything.
  5. Hints to the questions will be released on the discourse forum based on certain factors. The hints and even the titles of the question may not have any apparent meaning until you figure of the answer and/or receive another part of the clue.
  6. The Group is the quickest way to reach the moderators of the Treasure Hunt. You may ask for hints on the forum.
  7. The questions might involve literary references, simple cryptography, wordplay, steganography, conventional puzzles or maybe simple connect questions.
